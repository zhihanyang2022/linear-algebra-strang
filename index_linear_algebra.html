Lecture 4: LU and LDU factorization [PDF]

  • Inverse of AB
  • Inverse of \( A^{T} \)

Lecture 5: Tranposes, permutations, vector space, subspace, column space [PDF]

  • Permutation matrices: definition, pre-multiply for row exchanges, \( P^{-1} = P^{T} \)
  • \( PA = LU \) (I also gave two examples in JAX)
  • Tranpose: definition, symmetric matrices, \( R^T R \) is always symmetric
  • Vector space: set of vectors that's closed under linear combination
  • Subspace: vector space within a vector space, examples in \(R^2\) and \(R^3\)
  • Combinations of columns a matrix form a subspace called the column space

Lecture 6: Column space and nullspace [PDF]

  • Union and intersection of subspaces
  • \( Ax = b\) has solution if \( b \in C(A) \)
  • Nullspace: the set of all solutions of \( Ax = 0 \)

Lecture 7: Solving \(Ax = 0\): pivot variables, special solutions [PDF]

  • \( Ax = 0 \) => \( Ux = 0 \) (\(U\) is called row-echelon form) => \(Rx = 0\) (\(R\) is called reduced row-echelon form (RREF))
    • Row-echelon form: zero below pivots
    • Reduced row-echelon form: zero below and above pivots (from row operations upwards)
  • By putting the RREF form back as a sytem of equations, one can write x purely in terms of free variables. Doing so can help us recognize a basis (also called the "special solutions") for the nullspace.
  • Structure of RREF and the matrix of special solutions (N) when x is organized as pivot variables above free variables
