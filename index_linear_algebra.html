MIT 18.06 Linear Algebra

Lecture 1: Geometry of linear equations [PDF]

  • Fundamental problem of linear algebra: solving m equations with n unknowns
  • Row and column picture of a 2x2 system Ax=b
  • Row and column picture of a 3x3 system Ax=b
  • Can I solve Ax=b for every b? In other words, do the combinations of columns of A fill R^3? If not, how could it go wrong?
  • Ax is a linear combination of the columns of A

Lecture 2: Elimination with matrices [PDF]

  • Elimination and backsubstitution for solving a 3x3 system (very natural idea)
  • Vector-matrix product as combination of rows in the matrix
  • Elimination in matrix notation (same 3x3 system as above)

Lecture 3: Matrix operations and inverses [PDF]

  • 4 ways of looking at matrix-matrix multiplication; block multiplication
  • Definition of inverse; finding the inverse is like solving n systems (A is nxn)
  • Gauss-Jordan elimination (solving n equations at once) E[A I] = [I A^{-1}]

Lecture 4: LU and LDU factorization [PDF]

  • Inverse of AB
  • Inverse of \( A^{T} \)
  • Elimination as A=LU instead of EA=L; one 2x2 example; one 3x3 example showing the advantage of this representation
  • Elimination (i.e., finding LU; just record the row operations in L and the result as U) is \( \mathcal{O} (n^3) \); afterwards solving for different b's is \( \mathcal{O} (n^2) \)
